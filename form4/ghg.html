Human Impact on Natural Disasters

🌲 Deforestation

Removing vegetation increases erosion, faster water flow on concrete surfaces

🦆 Wetland Destruction

Wetlands absorb excess water - their loss leads to severe flooding

🚜 Agriculture

Depletes topsoil, weakens vegetation, reduces water absorption

🏭 Greenhouse Gases

Atmospheric components that trap heat, contributing to global temperature rise:

💨

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

From combustion, respiration, fermentation, decomposition

🔥

Methane (CH₄)

Natural gas, wetlands, oceans, produced by termites

⚡

Nitrogen Oxides (NO, NO₂)

Lightning, fossil fuel combustion

🏭

Sulphur Dioxide (SO₂)

Power plants, metal ore extraction

🛡️ The Ozone Layer

Region in Earth's stratosphere, 20-30 km above surface, absorbing most harmful UV radiation.

🌞 Importance

  • • Absorbs 93-99% of UV light
  • • Protects against harmful radiation
  • • Prevents health risks from UV exposure

⚠️ UV Exposure Risks

  • • Skin cancer
  • • Crop damage
  • • Harm to aquatic life

💥 Ozone Layer Depletion

🧪 Primary Cause: CFCs

Chlorofluorocarbons from coolants, foams, aerosol products

⚗️ Chemical Process

O₃ → O₂ + [O]
O₃ + [O] → 2O₂

📈 Health Effects

  • • Increased skin cancer risk
  • • Cataract formation
  • • Weakened immune system
  • • Premature skin aging
  • • Respiratory issues

🌿 Environmental Impact

  • • Decline in amphibian populations
  • • Disruption of food chains and biogeochemical cycles
  • • Alteration of plant physiological processes

🗑️ Waste Management

Waste: Unwanted materials produced by human activities

📋 By Physical State

Solid: Food scraps, paper, plastics
Liquid: Sewage, wastewater
Gaseous: Industrial emissions
Sludge: Wastewater treatment byproduct

🔄 By Degradability

Biodegradable

Decay quickly due to microbial action (food scraps, yard waste)

Non-Biodegradable

Don't decompose naturally (plastics, glass)

🏠

Domestic

Food leftovers, plastics, metals, sewage

🏭

Industrial

Used oil, toxic gases, metals

🌾

Agricultural

🏥

Medical

🧪

Laboratory
